Review of the Related Literature

According to the website of Gaboplywood ,Particle board, also known as chipboard, is an engineered wood product manufactured from wood chips, sawmill shavings, or even saw dust, and a synthetic resin or other suitable binder, which is pressed and extruded.

Particle board is a composite material and it is also cheaper, denser and more uniform than conventional wood and plywood and is substituted for them when appearance and strength are less important than cost. This is according to the webpage with a URL https://www.facebook.com/notes/thom-signature-design/carpentry-work/383157068375144/ They can be used as internal and external walls, ceilings, cabinets, doors, kitchen counters, and flooring for upper floors. Different grades of particle boards have different densities, with higher density connoting greater strength and greater resistance to failure of screw fasteners.

The researchers asked question to the www.answers.com about the chemicals used in making particle board got the following answers:

Chemicals such as CaCl2, Calcimine, Polymer Resin and Cement are being used in the manufacture of particle board. Accelerator (CaCl2) is a white deliquescent compound used chiefly as a drying agent, a refrigerant, and preservative and for controlling (www.answers.com). Calcimine is a white or tinted wash of zinc, glue, and water for ceilings and walls (Thesaurus). Polymer resin is a clear liquid plastic product that hardens the particle board to create a thick, durable, glossy coating (www.wisegeek.com). Once hardened, it is fade-proof and water resistant. White cement is a hydraulic binder. It is distinguished by its pure white color. This whiteness is the result of nature, as well as the rigorous selection of raw materials used in manufacturing and a technologically advanced production process. (http://www.lafarge.com/en/white-cements)
